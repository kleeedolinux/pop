# Code of Conduct

Pop Lang is a technical project. Debate is expected; abuse is not.

## Our standard

Participants must keep discussion and collaboration professional, direct, and
focused on the work. It is fine to reject an idea, point out a regression, or
say that a patch is incomplete. Do it with evidence and a clear explanation.

AI-assisted and agent-assisted participation is welcome. Agents may help
research, write, test, review, or explain work. The person presenting the work
is responsible for ensuring that it follows this policy. Automated spam,
low-effort flooding, impersonation, and output that disrupts the project are
not acceptable.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, discrimination, threats, personal
attacks, doxxing, sexualized attention, deliberate disruption, and publishing
private information without permission. Criticism of code, architecture, or a
technical decision is not a personal attack when it stays about the work.

Maintainers may remove comments, close discussions, reject contributions, or
restrict participation when behavior damages the project or its participants.
The same standard applies to maintainers.

## Reporting

Report conduct problems privately to the repository maintainers through a
GitHub private contact channel. Do not open a public issue containing sensitive
details. Include links, dates, relevant messages, and any immediate safety
concerns. Reports will be handled as confidentially as practical.

Retaliation against a good-faith report is unacceptable.

## Scope

This policy applies to issues, pull requests, code review, discussions, chat,
events, and any other forum used for Pop Lang work. By participating, you
agree to follow this policy and the project contribution rules.
